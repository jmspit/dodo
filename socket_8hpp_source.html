41  /**
42  * A Linux socket.
43  * Socket objects refer to, but do not own a particular socket descriptor. The programmer controls closing the socket
44  * by calling close(), the Socket destructor does not do this implicitly, as two Socket objects may refer to the
45  * same socket descriptor.
46  *
47  * The Socket operates either in blocking or non-blocking mode. In blocking mode, calls to listen() or receive()
48  * will block until data is received. Moreover, in blocking mode, there is no limit to the amount of data that can be
49  * sent or received in one go. In non-blocking mode, the send and receive sizes per call must fit in
50  * Socket::getSendBufSize() or Socket::getReceiveBufSize().
51  */
